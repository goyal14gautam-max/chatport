# Caffeine half-life research

**Source:** claude | **Type:** research | **Compressed 1,679 -> 807 chars**

## Goal
What is the typical half-life of caffeine in adults, and what factors cause it to vary?

## Findings
- The mean elimination half-life of caffeine in healthy adults is around 5 hours, though it ranges widely from 1.5 to 9.5 hours according to studies in the Annual Review of Nutrition.
- A controlled study published in the Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine found that 400 mg of caffeine consumed 6 hours before bedtime reduced total sleep time by more than 1 hour compared to placebo.
- Self-report tools exist but are imprecise.
- Direct CYP1A2 genotyping is the only reliable method.
